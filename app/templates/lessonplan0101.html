The Big Picture: Why us and why now?

Lesson Plan - Week 1, Day {{shortcode}}

Projected date: Monday April 14, 2014
Instructor(s): Fox, Rahman, Wiggins, Unger



Learning outcomes

After this lesson, participants will be able to:

  • Narrate how personal experiences and community identities brought them to this course, and describe what they want it to mean for their individual and communities' futures
  • Identify a social issue that affects people close to them, and discuss its race/class/gender/citizenship/sexuality/ability implications
  • Describe how that issue might be addressed through technology that helps to build or transfer power
  • Explain course goals, expectations, and format
  • Sign into their codeforprogress.org email account and send an email with an attached photo
  • Create an integer variable and a string variable
  • Store, replace, and add together integer and string variables (no lists or tuples yet)

Prep required before start of lesson

  • SmarTRIP cards, Metro intro, housing/here's-the-grocery-store conversations and work needs to be done before this so students can get to class
  • Remind students to bring their show-and-tell object/photo from home
  • Load software onto computers

Materials to bring (instructional team)

Note that whiteboards, easel pads, markers, projector, and screen should be in place for all lessons

  • Student computers(10)
  • USB drives (10)
  • Course text (10 copies)

Materials to bring (participants)

Note that participants should bring their computers, USB drives, notebooks, and course text every day

  • Show-and-tell object/photo from home
