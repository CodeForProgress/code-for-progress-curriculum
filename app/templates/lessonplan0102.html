Setting Up for (a) Movement: Staying healthy and happy while we learn and work

Lesson Plan - Week 1, Day {{shortcode}}

Projected date: Tuesday April 14, 2014
Instructor(s): Fox, Rahman, Wiggins



Learning outcomes

After this lesson, participants will be able to:

  • Describe the role and impact of previous mentors in their life
  • Describe the supporting impact they have had on someone in their life, the specific things they did to support, and how they learned to do those things
  • Explain the concept of an "ethic of care" (care as a leadership practice that we do vs. only something we feel), and as a "hard skill" for social justice organizing
  • Explain and critique the "soft skills"/"hard skills" dichotomy in social justice tech/field/data, and understand its gender and race implications
  • Write a list of more-than-basic wellness needs, and know where to locate them in Washington, DC
  • Articulate five personal/professional development goals they want to work on during the program
  • Assemble a personal development plan
  • Make, move, and delete folders and simple text files using the command line

Prep required before start of lesson

  • Collect and upload recommender videos, set up sound
  • Research on events this week/end and DC spots (see materials)

Materials to bring (instructional team)

Note that whiteboards, easel pads, markers, projector, and screen should be in place for all lessons

  • DC maps and lists for neighborhood joints, off-the-beaten-track spots, and places for wellness and fun
  • Recommender video files
  • Personal/professional development plan templates
  • File draw materials for command line exercise

Materials to bring (participants)

Note that participants should bring their computers, USB drives, notebooks, and course text every day

  • No special participant materials for this lesson.
